About Me - Ada Lovelace

Introduction

Ada Lovelace was a mathematician and writer, known for her work on Charles Babbage's early mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine.

Early Life and Death

Ada was Born on December 10, 1815, in London, England, she was the daughter of the poet Lord Byron. Her education in mathematics and logic paved the way for her contributions to the world of computing. Ada lived a very short life and died at the age of 36 on 27 November 1852, from uterine cancer.

Fun Facts

Ada Lovelace was tutored in mathematics from a young age, which was uncommon for women in the 19th century. She had a deep interest in both the arts and sciences, reflecting the influence of her famous poet father and her analytical mother. Ada corresponded with and was influenced by many prominent scientists and thinkers of her time, including Charles Babbage and Mary Somerville.
